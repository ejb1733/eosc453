Introduction

A course in the analysis and processing of data is of absolutely central importance in a majority of disciplines. In Geophysics, our observations of the earth, our data, are often gathered in  the form of signals.  These signals can be manipulated to extract information from them.  A particularly important task, is the identification and separation of signal from noise.  This quest is application dependent, because what is signal to some applications may be noise to others.  Also, signal and noise vary in time and frequency content from discipline to discipline. Different transformations and filters are designed with this in mind. A deep comprehension of how transforms and
filters work often allows one the extraction of useful information from noisy data.

These signals are often recorded as a discrete set of numbers over time, in which case we call them time series. This course deals mainly with the analysis of time series. The same techniques can be use for any series of numbers, independently of the independent variable utilized.
The principles and techniques that we will explore here have application to fields such as astronomy, economics, statistics, electrical engineering, etc.

A very important ingredient of any course is the book.
There is no "ideal" book for this course, because most text books on signal processing are written for electrical engineers, rather than Geophysicists.  However, following a book makes the course easier  for everybody. Therefore, we will use the following book,

Signal Processing and Linear Systems by B.P. Lathi (Berkeley-Cambridge).

We will use most of this book in detail, some parts in less detail and very few parts not at all. The book contains many exercises in Matlab.  Students do not need any prior knowledge of Matlab. The introductory lecture in the first laboratory period will give you all the required ammunition and will allow you to master this important language placing a very important tool at your disposal.
